Nikujaga

Nikujaga - A delicious Japanese meat and potato stew!

Nikujaga is a Japanese dish that literally means "meat and potatoes" in English. It is a type of stew made with sliced beef, potatoes, onions, and carrots cooked in a sweetened soy sauce and mirin (a sweet rice wine) broth. Other ingredients such as shirataki noodles, green beans, and mushrooms may also be added depending on the recipe. Nikujaga is a popular comfort food in Japan and is often served as a main dish with rice and miso soup.

Ingredients

  • Sliced beef
  • Potatoes
  • Onions
  • Carrots
  • Shirataki noodles (optional)
  • Green beans (optional)
  • Shiitake mushrooms (optional)
  • Dashi (Japanese soup stock)
  • Soy sauce
  • Mirin (sweet rice wine)
  • Sugar
  • Vegetable oil
  • Salt
  • Water

Steps

  1. In a large pot or deep frying pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the sliced onion and cook until translucent.
  3. Add the sliced beef and cook until browned on all sides.
  4. Add the dashi, soy sauce, mirin, and sugar to the pot and bring to a boil.
  5. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and simmer for about 10-15 minutes.
  6. Add the potatoes, carrots, shirataki noodles (if using), and enough water to cover the ingredients.
  7. Cover the pot and simmer for about 20 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  8. Add the green beans and shiitake mushrooms (if using) and simmer for another 5-10 minutes.
  9. Taste the broth and adjust the seasoning with salt if needed.
  10. Serve hot with steamed rice and miso soup on the side.
  11. Enjoy!
